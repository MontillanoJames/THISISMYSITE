JINMENKEN

HALF DOG ,HALF HUMAN

Jinkenmen are persistent in Japanese folkore, yet there seems to be more to them than that.
Throughout the Edo Era, from 1603 to 1868, these human-faced dogs were often encountered and sighted by locals,
to the point that they were occasionally featured in the news publications of the time.
In addition to the sightings, jinkenmen were at times allegedly exhibited at misemono,
which were a type of Japanese carnival sideshow popular during the Edo Era.
These sideshows were somewhat of a cabinet of curiosities, typically featuring menageries of exotic animals,
mounted displays or mummified remains of bizarre creatures or monsters, gaffes, mystical artifacts, and all manner of the strange and bizarre.
Taxidermy specimens of jinkenmen were often seen on display at such shows and on occasion even live specimens were shown.
In such shows, the jinkenmen would be paraded about for all to see and became quite popular attractions.
It is not clear whether these were actual jinkenmen or regular dogs somehow altered to look like such through
illusion and trickery, yet the fact remains that there are numerous accounts of these exhibitions and they were certainly
observed by many people.It was not only commoners that marveled at these creatures in such sideshows either.
